# Fellowship42 community code of conduct

Fellowship42 contributors, maintainers, and community participants must treat
one another with respect. We welcome thoughtful disagreement and evidence-
based criticism while protecting people from harassment and intimidation.

Expected conduct includes using inclusive language, assuming good intent until
evidence suggests otherwise, respecting privacy, accepting correction, and
keeping technical discussion focused on the work and its users.

Unacceptable conduct includes harassment, threats, discriminatory or sexualized
language, doxxing, publishing another person's private information, deliberate
disruption, retaliation, or sustained personal attacks.

This applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, project spaces, and
public events where someone represents Fellowship42. Maintainers may edit or
remove content, lock discussions, reject contributions, issue warnings, or
temporarily or permanently restrict participation according to severity and
pattern.

Report ordinary conduct problems privately to a repository or organization
maintainer through GitHub. Do not use the public issue tracker when a report
contains sensitive personal information. Maintainers will limit disclosure to
the people needed to assess and respond, subject to safety and legal duties.
