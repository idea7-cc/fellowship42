# Fellowship42 trademark policy

The Apache License 2.0 grants broad rights to use, modify, and distribute the
software, but it does not grant rights to use project names, logos, service
names, or certification marks beyond reasonable use in describing the origin
of the software and reproducing required notices.

This policy protects users from confusion while preserving the right to fork,
self-host, modify, and redistribute Fellowship42.

## Uses that do not require separate permission

You may:

- truthfully state that software is Fellowship42, is based on Fellowship42, or
  is compatible with a particular Fellowship42 release;
- retain the name in source, package metadata, copyright/license notices,
  unmodified release artifacts, and historical references;
- link to the official repository and discuss, review, teach, or report on the
  project; and
- describe yourself as a Fellowship42 user, contributor, consultant, or
  operator when that statement is accurate and does not imply endorsement.

Use the smallest amount of branding needed for those purposes. Make the actual
operator and source of a modified service clear.

## Uses that require written permission

Do not use Fellowship42 names or logos in a way that is likely to imply that a
fork, hosted service, domain, application listing, organization, commercial
offering, or event is official or endorsed. In particular, permission is
required to:

- call a service “Fellowship42 Cloud,” “Official Fellowship42,” or a confusingly
  similar name;
- use the Fellowship42 logo as the primary brand for a modified distribution or
  third-party hosted service; or
- claim “Fellowship42 Certified,” “Certified Fellowship42 Partner,” or another
  certification status outside the separately governed certification program.

The words “for Fellowship42,” “compatible with Fellowship42,” or “based on
Fellowship42” are generally clearer names for independent integrations and
services. Include a prominent statement that the offering is independently
operated and not endorsed by the Fellowship42 project unless permission says
otherwise.

## Forks and modified distributions

Code freedom is not conditional on branding permission. A fork may preserve
license notices and historical references, but its user-facing name and logo
must distinguish it when changes could cause users to believe it is an official
release. Do not remove attribution required by the license.

## Administration

The maintainers administering the `idea7-cc/fellowship42` repository interpret
this policy and may grant written exceptions. Ask through a non-confidential
repository issue; for confidential requests, contact an organization owner
privately through GitHub. This policy does not claim that every term is a
registered mark and is not legal advice.
